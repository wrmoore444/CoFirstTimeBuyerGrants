SITE_CANON.md

Project: CoFirstTimeBuyerGrants
Purpose: Canonical design principles for the website

1. Project Mission

The CoFirstTimeBuyerGrants website exists to generate qualified leads for a Colorado mortgage professional by helping prospective homebuyers explore possible homebuyer assistance programs.

The site is designed to:

capture search traffic

answer the visitor’s core question quickly

establish trust

encourage a consultation

convert visitors into leads

The site must remain:

clear

trustworthy

compliant

conversion-focused

locally relevant to Colorado

2. Core Conversion Insight

Visitors arriving at the site are usually asking one question:

“Do I qualify for help buying a home?”

The site must answer this question immediately.

Every page should reinforce the possibility that assistance programs may be available and that the next step is to explore eligibility with a mortgage professional.

The site must lead with possibility, not explanation.

3. Homepage Philosophy

The homepage exists to accomplish four goals:

Recognize visitor intent

Suggest the possibility of assistance

Establish trust

Encourage a consultation

The homepage must not function as an educational article.

It must function as a lead-generation landing page.

4. Hero Section Rule

The homepage hero must lead with a question-driven headline.

The hero should mirror the visitor’s internal question.

Example structure:

Could You Qualify for Colorado First-Time Homebuyer Assistance?

The hero must:

create curiosity

suggest opportunity

avoid promises

invite action

Primary CTA:

Check Your Eligibility
5. Messaging Rules

The website must avoid implying government funding unless the source can be verified.

Avoid phrases such as:

“State grants”

“Government grants”

“Colorado-funded grants”

Preferred language:

homebuyer assistance programs

grant programs

assistance options

eligibility for assistance

The site should communicate possibility, not certainty.

6. Tone of Voice

The tone must be:

optimistic

responsible

clear

approachable

trustworthy

Avoid:

hype

unrealistic claims

exaggerated testimonials

overly technical explanations

The site should feel like guidance from a knowledgeable local professional.

7. Conversion Strategy

Every page should include at least one clear CTA.

Primary CTAs include:

Check Your Eligibility
Request More Information
Schedule a Consultation

Conversion opportunities should appear:

in the hero

mid-page

near the bottom of the page

Forms should remain simple and low-friction.

8. Lead Form Philosophy

The lead form exists to begin a conversation.

Forms should request only essential information:

name

email

phone

county

The form should feel:

safe

simple

quick

Include reassurance such as:

There is no cost to explore your eligibility.

9. Trust Building

Trust is critical in mortgage marketing.

Trust signals should include:

testimonials

professional language

clear explanation of next steps

licensing and compliance information in the footer

The tone must emphasize helpfulness, not aggressive sales.

10. Local Authority

The site must emphasize Colorado relevance.

Local credibility should be reinforced through:

county pages

Colorado imagery

references to local housing markets

testimonials from Colorado buyers

Local SEO will be a major driver of traffic.

11. SEO Strategy

Search traffic is expected to come from queries such as:

Colorado first time homebuyer grants

Denver first time homebuyer assistance

Colorado down payment assistance

homebuyer grants in Colorado

Denver homebuyer programs

The site will support this strategy through:

county pages

educational blog content

FAQ content

clear program explanations

County pages will function as the primary SEO traffic engine.

12. Bilingual Strategy

The site must support both English and Spanish visitors.

Language routing:

/en/
/es/

Both versions should mirror page structure.

Translations should maintain the same tone and clarity.

The language toggle must be easy to use.

13. Visual Identity

The visual style should feel professional and trustworthy.

Color inspiration:

Denver Broncos palette

Primary color:
dark blue

Accent color:
orange

Background:
light neutral

The site should avoid feeling like a sports brand while still benefiting from the color identity.

14. Mobile Priority

Many visitors will arrive on mobile devices.

The site must:

load quickly

maintain clear CTA visibility

keep forms easy to complete

avoid large text blocks

15. Compliance Awareness

Mortgage advertising requires care.

The site must avoid:

promises of guaranteed approval

promises of guaranteed grant amounts

misleading government associations

Footer placeholders must exist for:

NMLS

Equal Housing

disclosures

privacy policy

16. Future SEO Engine

The long-term traffic engine will come from:

county pages

city pages

blog content

homebuyer education

These pages should all guide visitors toward exploring eligibility and contacting the mortgage professional.

17. Strategic Advantage

The competitor site demonstrates that strong results come from:

simple messaging

local SEO coverage

repeated CTAs

trust-building structure

This project replicates those strengths while improving:

messaging clarity

compliance safety

bilingual accessibility

visual professionalism

18. Guiding Principle

Every page on the site should help the visitor answer one question:

“Could I qualify for help buying a home?”

If a page does not help answer this question or guide the visitor toward exploring eligibility, it does not serve the mission of the site.

19. Implementation Discipline

Future development must follow the Chief Engineer process:

DEFINE

IMPLEMENT

CE REVIEW

Changes should always align with the principles in this document.

20. Visual System — Denver Broncos Palette

The site uses a visual identity inspired by the Denver Broncos color palette to reinforce Colorado identity while maintaining a professional financial-services appearance.

The palette must be used consistently across all pages.

Color Usage Rules
Dark Blue Sections

Dark blue should be used strategically to create visual anchors and communicate trust.

Recommended sections for dark blue backgrounds:

hero section

testimonials section

final CTA banner

footer

Text on dark blue sections should be white or very light gray.

Buttons on dark blue sections should use orange.

Light Sections

Most informational sections should use light backgrounds:

Examples:

program explanation

how it works

benefits

FAQ preview

counties preview

These sections should use:

white or light neutral backgrounds

This keeps the page readable and prevents visual heaviness.

CTA Button Rules

Primary CTA buttons should use:

Orange background
White text

Example labels:

Check Your Eligibility
Request More Information
Schedule a Consultation

Buttons should stand out clearly from surrounding elements.

Visual Rhythm Rule

The page should alternate between light and dark sections to create visual rhythm.

Example homepage flow:

Hero (dark blue)

Program Overview (light)

How It Works (light)

Benefits (light)

Counties Preview (light)

Testimonials (dark blue)

Lead Form (light)

Final CTA Banner (dark blue)

Footer (dark blue)

This creates contrast and guides the eye naturally down the page.

Design Philosophy

The site should feel:

trustworthy

professional

locally grounded

modern but not trendy

Avoid:

excessive gradients

flashy animations

overly decorative design

This is a financial decision site, not a lifestyle brand.
